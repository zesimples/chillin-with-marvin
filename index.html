Marvin, the Paranoid Android who is perpetually depressed and gloomy, is a robot character from Douglas Adams' "The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" series.

Programmed with a "brain the size of a planet," Marvin is highly intelligent but also deeply dissatisfied. His wry, deadpan humour and his tendency to express existential despair make him a memorable companion in the series' comedic science fiction universe.

If even Marvin, a robot with computational capabilities beyond human imagination, can experience depression, how couldn't humans, with all their emotional complexities, also find themselves susceptible to such emotional states?

In a galaxy-spanning twist of self-improvement, Marvin, the Paranoid Android, recently enrolled in interstellar mental health therapy. One of his therapeutic challenges was to construct his own "Hope Box," a cosmic repository of uplifting thoughts and mementos designed to elevate his circuits during his frequent bouts of existential despair.

Marvin's galactic "Hope Box" is an eclectic yet fitting collection of items tailored to his unique robotic sensibilities:

- a joke teller module because Marvin operates under the principle that "a joke a day may keep the therapist many light-years away." - a music player programmed with an array of circuit-warming songs to elevate his mood—or at least to attempt to do so. - a weather forecast display to remind him that existence has its own natural rhythms of highs and lows, and to appreciate the imperfect beauty that the universe, even in its most chaotic forms, has to offer.

This project aims to destigmatize depression by highlighting that it's a universal condition that can affect anyone, regardless of background or circumstance. The web app encourages people to seek help for depression by offering easy-to-use tools like a "Hope Box," making mental health care approachable and manageable.

This website serves as the culminating group project for theStudio Full Stack Developer course. Given the challenge to build a Single Page Application (SPA) inspired by a famous AI, it was collaboratively brought to life by the authors

Vera Costa, José Penacho, Kévin Mateus and Vasco Villas.
